In the majority of low and middle-income nations, newborn jaundice is a major factor in neonatal admissions and mortality. It causes a baby's skin and eyes to turn yellow. Jaundice in newborns is fairly prevalent and can happen when bilirubin levels are high in newborns. Increased bilirubin, if untreated, can harm the brain. To identify babies who are at risk for hyperbilirubinemia, early jaundice prediction has become increasingly important. In order to assist the health sector, our research is proposing a method to develop a non-invasive method for detecting jaundice on a regular basis to estimate bilirubin. Therefore, we suggest a machine learning approach for non-invasive early neonatal jaundice prediction. In this research, to classify the risk level of jaundice as jaundice and non jaundice we created a prediction model employing identified classification parameters. We gather data from the Bangabandhu Memorial Hospital. A predictive model for classifying the risk levels of jaundice was developed using data from 250 neonates and 2 classification factors that were found in neonates. 80% of the dataset is used for training, and the remaining 20% is used for testing. Also utilized is 10-fold cross-validation. Following data organization and pre-processing.
